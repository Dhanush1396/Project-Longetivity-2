# SOP: Compile Study Material

## Purpose
Transform scraped sources into a focused 30-min study document.

## Structure (HTML Email)
```
HEADER: Day N | Topic Title | Date
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. OVERVIEW (~300 words)
   - What this topic is and why it matters for longevity
   - Key question this session answers

2. THE SCIENCE (~800–1200 words)
   - Core mechanisms and definitions
   - Key studies cited inline [Source: Author, Year, URL]
   - Data, statistics, findings

3. CURRENT STATE OF RESEARCH (~600–800 words)
   - Where the science stands today
   - Debates / open questions
   - Recent breakthroughs (past 1–2 years)

4. PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS (~400–600 words)
   - What this means for a longevity-focused person
   - Evidence-based actions (if any)
   - What NOT to do based on the evidence

5. KEY TAKEAWAYS (bullet list)
   - 5–7 crisp points

6. CITED RESOURCES
   - Full list with URLs, type, and one-line description
   - Organized by: Journals | Articles | Videos/Podcasts

7. NEXT SESSION PREVIEW
   - Tomorrow's topic and why it builds on today
```

## Tone Rules
- Academic & dense — no dumbing down
- Cite everything — [Author, Year] inline, full URL in resources
- No pseudoscience, no supplement recommendations
- Use precise terminology, define on first use

## Length Target
2,500–4,000 words (approx 25–40 min reading at 100 words/min)
